Structured giving for measurable impact

We dedicate one third of our philanthropy to local Redwood City & Bay Area partners, one third to national priorities, and one third to international impact. This structure keeps us proactive, ensures balanced outcomes, and helps each grant build capacity for the communities we care about.

Why we give with structure

Regular, structured giving is a habit, not a finish line. Spiritual traditions across Christianity, Islam, Sikhism, Judaism, and others have long recommended setting aside roughly ten percent of income to care for community. We follow that wisdom, pair it with data, and commit to realistic percentages we can maintain year after year.

Start early, stay consistent, compare notes.

We began structured giving right out of college. Setting a fixed portion of every paycheck--even when they were small--made support for others a default decision. We also talk about giving with friends and colleagues, not to compare totals, but to swap ideas on high-impact causes and keep each other accountable.

Philanthropy observers often note that Silicon Valley households donate a smaller share of income than national averages. New wealth can arrive before a giving habit forms. We want to normalize talking about philanthropic strategy in tech circles, encourage first-jobs donors to start small, and show how an intentional plan keeps impact from becoming another "someday" project.

Our north star is simple: thoughtful giving should feel approachable. The goal of this site is to share what works for us--clear priorities, transparent partners, and honest reflections--so you can adapt it for your own values.

Impact highlights

These projects stretch far beyond a single grant. They demonstrate what long-term partnership, local knowledge, and scientific rigor can accomplish.

Investing in education with JAAGO Foundation

We funded the land purchase for two JAAGO Foundation school sites in Bangladesh: Teknaf in Cox's Bazar District and a new campus in Dhaka. Together they will serve more than 1,500 children each year with evidence-based, locally led instruction that keeps students on a path to secondary education and stable employment.

Locations: Teknaf School (Google Maps) | Dhaka campus (breaking ground 2025, opening 2026 for 1,100 students)

The Dhaka school expands JAAGO's reach into the capital where the waitlist is longest. Once construction completes, it will welcome 1,100 children with wraparound services that include nutrition, counseling, and after-school tutoring.

Backing pediatric brain cancer breakthroughs

Through the Lucile Packard Foundation for Children's Health, we underwrite a postdoctoral researcher--Michael--at the Monje Lab at Stanford . The lab is pioneering treatments for diffuse intrinsic pontine glioma (DIPG) and other pediatric brain tumors.

Recent publications from the team that inspire our support:

  • Cell: Emerging therapeutic targets for pediatric high-grade gliomas (2025)
  • Nature: Single-cell insights into tumor-microenvironment interactions (2024)
  • Cell: Neuroimmune pathways in DIPG response (2025)

Funding researchers directly allows the lab to pursue bold, longitudinal studies and gives families fresh hope for better therapies. That visit with Dr. Monje inspired our son Max to pursue biochemistry so he can contribute to neuro-oncology breakthroughs.

Standing with families facing cancer

Both of our families have lost loved ones to cancer--lung, prostate, and brain tumors in particular. We fund national organizations that accelerate research, provide patient navigation, and close the gap between diagnosis and effective care, including the Lung Cancer Research Foundation, Lung Cancer Foundation of America, LUNGevity Foundation, Prostate Cancer Foundation, Zero -- The End of Prostate Cancer, and the National Brain Tumor Society. These partners publish measurable outcomes, from clinical trial enrollment to survivorship support hours, helping us track progress year over year.

How we give (and how you can, too)

A Donor Advised Fund (DAF) lets us automate contributions, invest tax-efficiently, and time grants thoughtfully. Below are the providers we point friends to most often when they want to get started quickly.

  • Schwab Charitable: open a donor-advised fund -- step-by-step overview, contribution requirements, and investment pools.
  • Vanguard Charitable: compare funds and onboard online -- a good fit for index-focused investors or existing Vanguard clients.
  1. Open a DAF: Compare custodians (Schwab, Vanguard, Fidelity, or your community foundation). Look for transparency on minimum contributions, fees, and investment options.
  2. Automate contributions: We transfer a fixed percentage of income into the DAF each month. Payroll automation removes friction and keeps commitments on track.
  3. Document your filters: We score potential grants on mission alignment, leadership, evidence of impact, and financial health. A short checklist beats trying to make decisions ad hoc.
  4. Rebalance annually: Every winter we review grants to ensure our one-third allocations still hold and adjust for new priorities.
  5. Talk about it: Share your giving framework with peers. The goal is to swap ideas and celebrate impact--not to create a leaderboard.

Questions we hear

Why share your giving publicly?

Transparency keeps us accountable and, we hope, encourages others to map out their own giving strategies. This isn't victory laps--it's an invitation to a conversation about what's working and what still needs support.

Are you soliciting donations or grant requests?

No. We're not seeking pitches or applications. This site exists to explain our philosophy and inspire structured giving elsewhere. If you'd like encouragement or resources for your own plan, we're glad to chat.

How much should someone start with?

Pick a percentage that feels meaningful but sustainable--maybe 3% while you pay off loans, or 10% if you're ready to tithe. Automate it, review annually, and increase as your capacity grows.

How do you vet organizations?

We combine on-the-ground familiarity, diligence through Charity Navigator and GuideStar, impact reports, and candid conversations with leadership teams. We'll publish a one-sentence mission and reference links for every partner as we finish documenting them.

Can smaller donors make a difference?

Absolutely. Regular contributions--no matter the size--let nonprofits plan. Even $25 a month through a DAF or direct recurring gift adds up quickly and keeps you engaged with the impact you care about.
